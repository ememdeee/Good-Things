Frequently Asked Questions

A Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) is a unique 17-character code assigned to every vehicle. It serves as the vehicle's fingerprint, providing essential information about its make, model, year, and manufacturing details. You can find the VIN on the dashboard, near the windshield, or inside the driver's door jamb.

A car history report includes detailed information about the vehicle's past, including accident history, service records, ownership changes, mileage readings, and title status. It also shows if the vehicle has been involved in any major incidents or has outstanding liens.

A vehicle report helps you make informed decisions by revealing potential issues, verifying mileage accuracy, confirming ownership history, and identifying any previous damage or accidents. This information can help you avoid costly surprises and negotiate a fair price.

While some basic vehicle information may be available for free, comprehensive vehicle history reports typically require a fee. This is because they compile data from multiple sources and provide detailed information about the vehicle's past.
