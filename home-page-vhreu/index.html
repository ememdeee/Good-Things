What is a VIN Number?

A VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) is a 17-character string of letters and numbers assigned to each vehicle by the manufacturer. The VIN can be found on the dashboard of your vehicle or on the driver's side door and is unique to each vehicle. With a vehicle's VIN, you can gain access to every detail of a used vehicle, and thoroughly check its history. Some of the details you can uncover with the VIN includes auction/sales history, odometer rollbacks, ownership history, open recalls and others.

What We Offer

Free VIN Decoding

For no cost at all, you can carry out VIN decoding of your vehicle to gain access to the vehicle's make, model, trim, year, engine description, country and other information, all for free. When you decode your VIN, you reduce the risk of purchasing a damaged vehicle from the dealers as you have firsthand information and history on the vehicle. Also, a decoded VIN reduces the time needed to identify or confirm the features and specifications of the vehicle.

Vehicle History Report

By generating a Vehicle History Report for your vehicle, you can discover accurate accident and damage records, auction history, open recalls, theft records, and more.
